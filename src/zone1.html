Zone 1 - Easy Basic Privacy

Zone 1 is compromised of steps that everyone with a smartphone, tablet, or computer should be taking. These are very basic steps that do not take much time or effort to do and go a very long way in protecting your online identity. Here are the things you should do on your devices to ensure maximum security in zone 1.

Password Manager

1. Make DIFFERENT secure passwords for all your accounts
2. NEVER use the same password twice because you're basically saving the hacker some extra work that he would be doing to get into your accounts
3. Test how secure your password using the password tester provided in the links page
4. NEVER answer security question truthfully. Always use random answers and write them down in your password manager

Web Browser

1. Use 2-Factor authentication and if possible use google authenticator, if not use your phone
2. You can use any browser in zone 1 BUT turn off any website data collection, cookies, or location trackers
3. Only use trusted add-ons
4. Use a paid VPN not the free ones

Social Media

1. Hide real information on social media
2. DO NOT talk to strangers
3. DO NOT post in public comments, that could bring about unwanted attention
4. Disable permissions social media applications do not need such as location, SMS, etc.
5. Limits pictures of yourself
6. Never put in your phone number unless absolutely needed

Smartphones & Computers

1. DO NOT use short pin or easy password. Use a password with letters(upper and lowecase), numbers, and symbols
2. Disable setting that send usage data to the app
3. Activate paid VPN
4. Disable bluetooth
5. Remove apps you don't use

Data Privacy Statistics:

1. Every 2 seconds there is a new victim of identity theft.

Nearly 60 million Americans have been affected by identity theft, according to a 2018 online survey by The Harris Poll.

Identity theft causes billions of dollars in total losses. The amount stolen from victims of identity theft was over $16 billion in 2017.
